Communication

HalWorld

The Halliburton intranet, Halworld, is home to a wealth of useful and helpful information, which includes policies, procedures, global announcements and press releases. There are numerous links to information relating to Support Services such as Human Resources and Health and Safety.

As a new entrant we encourage you to browse Halworld during your induction period. If you are not sure where to look then please ask our local HRD representative.

HRD/ Check-ins

As part of your induction you will be introduced to our HRD representative. There are many development options available to employees and you can discuss with them any training requirements or career aspirations.

You should also have regular check-ins with your supervisor, this is an informal chat about how your work is progressing and if you have any additional needs. The check-in process should be driven by you. If this has not already been covered you can ask either HR or your supervisor to discuss it further.

Safety Briefings

Each month safety briefings are held in the briefing room, middle floor of the office block.

In Halliburton safety is paramount and we pride ourselves in Arbroath for our excellent safety record. Halliburton are involved in a number of safety initiatives

Through the briefings we discuss different safety topics and complete training. In addition we also discuss how the plant is performing.

Continuous Improvement

Continuous improvement initiatives are what drives our business. From strategic planning to plant specific goal setting. You will definitely see our CI team around and about, if you want to know more and are interested in getting involved in any kaizen events please ask your supervisor.

You may also notice a matrix displayed in the main area outside the main machine shop. This outlines our goals for the year and drives all activity within the facility.

If you would like to know more, you can arrange a tour with our CI Lead

Keeping Informed

Communication plays a big part in how Halliburton engages with their employees. Throughout the Arbroath facility there are wall boards, stands and wall mounted monitors displaying important and useful information. It is you responsibility to read any notices displayed on these boards.

Front line employees will also receive daily toolbox talks to communicate relevant organisational or local site news and information.

Every Halliburton employee will be provided with a company email address and account. You should check your email regularly for global and local information. It is possible to access your email from a non-Halliburton device, please ask our HRD representative to get you set up with access, and while you are at it, get your printer setup. Your badge enables you to print from any secure printer in the facility.

Shop floor Etiquette

During your shift you are entitled to 2 x 10 minute breaks. During your on-boarding period these will be at the same time as your mentor. However once you have your shift pattern these will be scheduled for you.

No food is allowed on the shop floor and any drinks must have a lid to prevent spillage.

Personal mobile phones, except for in an emergency, should not be used at any time on the shop floor. These should be kept in your locker.
